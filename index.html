A little about me:

I was born in Dickinson, North Dakota, and a long series of events led to me being raised in Oshkosh, Wisconsin for most of my childhood. I've always been intrigued by science and curious to learn new things, and as I grew older I joined my middle school's LEGO League team. I loved robotics, but my coaches noticed that I was quite deft when it came to programming the little robot to do the necessary actions to complete its task. I didn't think much of it, until they gave me an award at the end of the season, commending me for my contributions. They didn't know, but that moment was what brought me to where I am today: a sophomore in Software Engineering at NDSU, part of the ACM and Byttle Revamp team, an avid coder in my freetime, and someone who is endlessly astounded by the vast amount of possibilities that lay literally at my fingertips. I can't thank them enough.
